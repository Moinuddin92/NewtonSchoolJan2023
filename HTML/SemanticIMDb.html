Plot

In 1951, the government officials, accompanied by an influential crime boss Suryavardhan, discover a gold ore in southern part of the Mysore state while on the same day, a poverty-stricken woman Shanti gives birth to a baby boy who is named Raja Krishnappa Bairya. Suryavardhan has the officials assassinated, leases the land for 99 years under the pretext of mining limestone from the land, which he names Narachi but covertly establishes K.G.F.

Yash as Raja Krishnappa Bairya "Rocky" [12] a young boy who grew up in poverty after his mother's death; he becomes a high-profile mercenary, working under the associates of KGF's cult, before rising as a liberator.

In an interview with The Times of India, Prashanth Neel stated that he planned to split the film in two parts, as the narration of the story is in a non-linear format. The decision to split the film into two parts also had to do with its commercial prospects. He further added “The scale of the project is huge and we had a scope for a beginning, an interval, and an end for both parts, so it made sense for us to release it as two parts”.

Critical response

K.G.F: Chapter 1 received positive reviews from critics.[142] While its story plot, cinematography, action sequences, music, narraton, direction and cast performances of the ensemble cast received acclaim, critics had mixed comments about the screenplay and editing. However the film's dialogues became famous worldwide, especially Yash and Anant Nag's dialogues.
